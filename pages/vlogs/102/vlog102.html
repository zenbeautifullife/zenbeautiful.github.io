Sketching 5 Unique Coral | Easy sketch Anyone Can Try!

Too busy to make art lately?

In this vlog, I’m sharing a calming sketching and watercolor session where I explore the beautiful shapes and textures of five different corals.
Inspired by the amazing variety of reef life, I started with some light pencil sketches, then outlined them using my fountain pen before adding color with watercolors.
This piece was all about observing natural forms, playing with ink lines, and using a limited palette to bring each coral to life.

My Process

I began by drafting the shapes in pencil to capture the structure of each coral. Then, I carefully outlined everything with a fountain pen—letting the lines stay loose and expressive, not too perfect. That took about five minutes and helped set the stage for the painting. After inking, I moved on to watercolors, giving each coral its own color story:
Staghorn Coral – painted with Bright Blue
Elkhorn Coral – Madder Lake Red Light at the top and Cadmium Orange at the base
Mystery Coral – a clustered coral I painted in Cerulean Blue
Tube Sponge – a lovely green mix using Cadmium Lemon
Lettuce Leaf Coral – soft green mixed with a touch of Cerulean Blue

Each coral has such a unique personality, and I loved using color to enhance that.
